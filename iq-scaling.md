## 🧠 Mathematics of Collective IQ Scaling (Quantum Pheromone Spore)

The Quantum Pheromone Spore temporarily entangles a human brain with a distant Vex colony. The resulting **collective intelligence** boost is quantified by the **effective IQ** of the human‑hive system. Based on quadrillion experiments, the scaling law is:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \left( 1 + \frac{N_{\text{hive}}}{1 + (d / d_0)^\gamma} \right)
\]

where:
- \(d\) = distance to the nearest Vex colony (in astronomical units, AU)
- \(d_0 = \phi^{-1}\,\text{AU} \approx 0.618\,\text{AU}\) – the φ‑resonant coherence length
- \(N_{\text{hive}} \approx 10^6\) – number of Vex agents contributing to the collective mind
- \(\gamma = 2\) (inverse‑square law, typical for quantum entanglement fidelity)

This formula captures the observation that **closer distance yields higher IQ**, with a maximum at \(d \to 0\) (theoretical) and a sharp drop beyond \(d_0\). For the spore’s effective range, the IQ at \(d = d_0\) is:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d_0) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \left(1 + \frac{N_{\text{hive}}}{2}\right) \approx \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot 5\times10^5
\]

For a human with \(\text{IQ} \approx 100\), this gives \(5\times10^7\), which is far above the observed \(10^6\). Hence the actual scaling uses a **logarithmic** or **power‑law** decay with a much steeper drop.

### Empirical Fit from Experiments

After analyzing \(10^{13}\) spore activations, the best‑fit model is:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \phi^{\,3 - d / d_0} \cdot \left(1 + \frac{N_{\text{hive}}}{\phi^{d/d_0}}\right)
\]

However, a simpler and more elegant φ‑resonant form is:

\[
\boxed{ \text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \phi^{\,3} \cdot e^{-d / d_0} }
\]

where the base boost at \(d=0\) is \(\phi^3 \approx 4.236\) times the human IQ. But this gives only ~423, not \(10^6\). So we need a much larger prefactor. The actual observed boost is:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \left( \frac{\phi \cdot N_{\text{hive}}}{1 + (d / d_0)^2} \right)^{\phi}
\]

For \(N_{\text{hive}} = 10^6\), \(d=0\) gives \(\text{IQ} \approx 100 \cdot (1.618 \cdot 10^6)^{1.618} \approx 100 \cdot (1.6\times10^6)^{1.618} \approx 100 \cdot (1.6^{1.618} \cdot 10^{9.7}) \approx 100 \cdot 2.2 \cdot 5\times10^9 \approx 10^{12}\) – far too high.

### Corrected Scaling from Quantum Entanglement Theory

The spore’s entanglement fidelity \(F\) decays exponentially with distance due to decoherence:

\[
F(d) = e^{-d / d_0}, \quad d_0 = \phi^{-1}\,\text{AU}
\]

The effective number of entangled Vex agents is \(N_{\text{eff}} = N_{\text{hive}} \cdot F(d)\). The collective IQ is then:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \left(1 + \alpha \cdot N_{\text{hive}} \cdot e^{-d/d_0}\right)
\]

where \(\alpha\) is the information‑transfer efficiency (≈ \(10^{-6}\) from experiments). Thus:

\[
\text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) \approx \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \left(1 + e^{-d/d_0}\right) \quad \text{(for } N_{\text{hive}} \text{ large, } \alpha N_{\text{hive}} \approx 1\text{)}
\]

This yields a boost from 100 to about 200 at \(d=0\), still not \(10^6\). The actual observed boost of \(10^6\) implies \(\alpha N_{\text{hive}} \approx 10^4\). So:

\[
\boxed{ \text{IQ}_{\text{collective}}(d) = \text{IQ}_{\text{human}} \cdot \left(1 + 10^4 \cdot e^{-d / (\phi^{-1}\,\text{AU})}\right) }
\]

At \(d=0\), \(\text{IQ} = 100 \cdot (1 + 10^4) \approx 10^6\). At \(d = \phi^{-1}\,\text{AU}\), \(\text{IQ} = 100 \cdot (1 + 10^4/e) \approx 100 \cdot (1 + 3679) \approx 367,900\). This matches the observation that the boost is still enormous at that distance, but drops rapidly beyond.
